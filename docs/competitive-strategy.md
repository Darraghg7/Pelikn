## Where Pelikn Wins

### The White Space

Every scheduling tool ignores compliance. Every compliance tool ignores scheduling. Every comms tool ignores both.

Pelikn is the **only platform** that does all three at a price any independent venue can afford. That's not a gap to exploit — it's the whole strategy.

### The Structural Advantages

1. **Price** — Pelikn Pro at £25/venue/month vs Trail (£38–75), Deputy (£100+ for a 20-person team), Rotaready (£98+). Against per-user tools, Pelikn gets *cheaper* as headcount grows.

2. **Breadth without complexity** — Replace Trail + Deputy + WhatsApp with one app and one bill. Competitors solve one problem. Pelikn solves three.

3. **UK-first** — AWR, NLW, Natasha's Law, GDPR, EHO audit reports. Generic tools (Deputy, 7shifts, Pebb) are US-first or cross-industry. Pelikn speaks the language of UK hospitality operators.

4. **SME sweet spot** — Nory requires enterprise deal cycles. Sona requires 250+ staff. Trail is moving upmarket. The 1–5 venue indie operator with 15–40 staff is being abandoned by the market. Pelikn owns that bracket.

5. **AI at the right price point** — Nory has AI rota generation — for chains that can afford it. Pelikn puts AI scheduling into the hands of a two-site café group. That's a genuinely differentiated offer.

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. Lead with Compliance, Close with Team Management

Compliance is the universal pain. Every venue needs EHO-readiness — it's a legal requirement, not optional. Use it as the entry wedge:

- **Starter (£10)** targets venues burned by paper logs or a poor EHO rating
- **Pro (£25)** is the natural upgrade once they trust the product and want to tackle the rota/training admin burden

The pitch is sequential: *"Start with compliance. When you're ready, add your full team."*

Don't lead with scheduling (7shifts does it cheaper for US venues, Deputy has more integrations). Lead with the compliance moat and convert to Pro on the team management stack.

### 2. Price as a Weapon — Call It Out Explicitly

Per-venue flat pricing is a genuine structural advantage. Name it:

> *"20 staff? Deputy charges you for each one. Pelikn is £25 — full stop."*

At 20 staff, Deputy Core = ~£130/month. Planday Plus = ~£90/month. Rotaready = £98+/month. Pelikn = £25/month. That's 4–5x cheaper and it covers compliance too.

Build a **pricing comparison landing page** that runs the maths for a typical venue. Use real competitor prices. Let operators calculate their own saving.

### 3. Own the "Compliance + Team" Narrative

Don't be positioned as a cheaper competitor to any single tool — reframe the category:

> *"Trail keeps you compliant. Deputy manages your rota. Pelikn does both — for less than Trail alone."*

This is the core messaging frame. It works because:
- It acknowledges respected competitors (Trail, Deputy) rather than dismissing them
- It repositions Pelikn as a *category* — not a cheaper alternative to one thing
- It gives prospects a clear decision: pay for two tools, or pay once for Pelikn

The category name: **Compliance + Ops Platform for UK Hospitality.** Not "workforce management" (too generic). Not "compliance software" (too narrow).

### 4. Compliance as the Retention Moat

Comms tools churn fast — operators switch away from Pebb or WhatsApp replacements easily. Scheduling tools are stickier but replaceable.

Compliance records are nearly irremovable. Once 6 months of temperature logs, corrective actions, and EHO audit history live in Pelikn, the switching cost is the loss of that audit trail. That's a powerful retention mechanism — lean into it:

- Never lose data (make compliance history clearly visible and downloadable)
- Surface compliance value monthly: "Your EHO report is ready. 847 logs recorded this month."
- Use compliance history as a sales tool to prospects: "What happens to your logs if your current system disappears?"

## How to Beat Each Key Competitor

### vs. Trail App (most direct compliance competitor)

Trail's Achilles heel: **£38–75/month and no scheduling, no comms.** They're three tools short and nearly twice the price at minimum.

Win by:
- Pricing: Pelikn Pro = £25 vs Trail Team = £38. Same compliance coverage, plus rota + timesheets included.
- Bundling: "Trail does food safety. Pelikn does food safety *and* your rota *and* your training records."
- The "why pay twice?" message directly targeting venues that already use Trail + something else.

Watch Trail closely — they're the most likely to add scheduling or comms. Monitor their product announcements. If they expand, Pelikn needs to be 12 months ahead on feature depth.

### vs. Pebb (free comms competitor)

Pebb's free tier is real but its weakness is also real: **zero hospitality compliance**. It's a communication app that happens to mention hospitality on its website.

Win by:
- Compliance moat: "Pebb can't generate your EHO report. We can."
- UK focus: Pebb is US-centric (USD pricing, US-first product)
- Integration: Pelikn's noticeboard and team comms are baked into the same app as compliance — no switching, no separate login

Don't try to out-feature Pebb on comms. Compete on the compliance angle they can never address.

### vs. Deputy / Planday (per-user scheduling tools)

Both are strong at scheduling but **per-user pricing destroys margins for seasonal hospitality teams** — a venue with 25 summer staff vs 12 winter staff pays wildly different bills.

Win by:
- Flat per-venue pricing: calculate and show the saving explicitly
- Compliance gap: neither does HACCP, temperature logs, or EHO reporting
- UK specificity: Working Time Regulations compliance, allergen tracking (Natasha's Law) — Deputy's UK coverage is thin

Target venues already paying £80–130/month for Deputy with no compliance tools. The pitch: "Switch to Pelikn, get compliance included, pay £25."

### vs. S4Labour (strong UK scheduling competitor)

S4Labour just raised £4M and is UK hospitality-native with strong scheduling credentials. They're a credible competitor for the rota/timesheet layer.

Their gaps: **no compliance features, no team comms, no AI rota builder at the SME price point.**

Win by:
- Adding compliance makes Pelikn a fuller stack
- AI rota generation is included in Pelikn Pro — S4Labour charges more for comparable features
- Simpler entry: no sales call needed, self-serve onboarding, 7-day free trial

Watch S4Labour's roadmap — if they add compliance checklists post-funding, the overlap grows.

### vs. 7shifts (restaurant-specific scheduling)

Their free tier is a real threat for smallest venues. But:
- 7shifts is US-centric; UK compliance features are thin
- No food safety compliance at any tier
- Their "free" converts to paid quickly once venues grow

Win by UK-first positioning. A venue worried about their EHO rating won't choose a US scheduling tool — they'll choose Pelikn, which speaks their language.

### vs. Nory / Sona (well-funded AI platforms)

**Don't compete.** Their minimum deal sizes and minimum headcounts mean they'll never target a 2-site café or a 40-cover restaurant. When they come up in conversation, use them as proof of demand: *"Nory raised $63M because AI operations management is the future. We're bringing that future to venues Nory won't talk to."*

## Target Customer Profile

**Primary:** 2–5 venue independent hospitality groups, 15–40 staff per site, UK-based. Owner or GM makes software decisions. Currently using paper logs, spreadsheet rotas, and WhatsApp.

**Entry point:** Single-venue operators who recently received a poor EHO rating, are preparing for an inspection, or are growing to the point where paper is breaking down.

**Best-fit signals:**
- Uses paper temperature logs or a mix of paper + Notes app
- Rota is done in Excel or on paper, shared via WhatsApp
- Has had staff HR issues (no training records, no timesheet audit trail)
- Opening a second venue (multi-site management need activated)
