Section 01
Who we're targeting
We're not going after every hospitality business. We're going after the ones who feel the compliance and admin burden most acutely - and have the authority to change it.
ICP 01 · Primary
The Independent Restaurant Owner
"I'm the one doing the temperature logs at 11pm because no one else knows where the clipboard is."
1–3 site operator. 10–25 staff. FSA-rated. Spending 8+ hours/week on admin. No dedicated HR or ops person - it all falls on them.
→ Decision maker, credit card buyer, immediate pain
ICP 02 · Secondary
The Multi-Site Group Manager
"I manage compliance for four sites and each one does it differently. There's no consistency and I can't audit any of them remotely."
3–10 site group. 30–100 staff. Mix of employed and zero-hours contracts. Needs centralised oversight. Has tried spreadsheets and found them lacking.
→ Longer sales cycle, higher ACV, strong referral potential
ICP 03 · Emerging
The New Venue Operator
"We open in six weeks. I need to know what compliance records I have to keep from day one - and I've got no idea where to start."
Pre-opening or recently opened. FSA registration in progress. Small team, limited budget, high anxiety about getting compliance right. No legacy systems to migrate from.
→ Easy conversion, grows with the product, strong LTV
Why timing matters
The best moment to reach a venue owner isn't when things are calm. It's the week after an EHO inspection, a bad Tripadvisor review citing food safety, or a payroll error that took four hours to untangle. That's when they're ready to change.
Our acquisition strategy is built around these trigger moments - identifying venues that have recently experienced a compliance event or operational pain point, and reaching them when the problem is still fresh.

Section 02
How we reach them
Four channels, each chosen because it reaches venue owners in moments of genuine need - not just browsing.
Channel 01 · Owned
Content & SEO
Venue owners search for help when they have a problem - "how to pass EHO inspection", "temperature log template UK", "Natasha's Law compliance." We meet them there with genuinely useful content that builds trust before the sale.
  • FSA inspection prep guides (gated downloads)
  • Natasha's Law allergen compliance toolkit
  • HACCP template library - free, ungated
  • "How to run a 5-star kitchen" blog series
  • Google Business targeting: "food safety software UK"
Investment: Content writer + SEO tooling · Low CAC, compounds over time
Channel 02 · Paid
Performance Marketing
Highly targeted Google Search and Meta campaigns aimed at hospitality operators actively searching for compliance or scheduling solutions. Tight audience segmentation - not spraying budget across broad keywords.
  • Google Search: compliance + scheduling intent keywords
  • Meta retargeting: engaged content readers
  • LinkedIn: hospitality group managers and operators
  • YouTube pre-roll: venue owner how-to content
  • Target CPA: <£120 (payback in <5 months)
Investment: £3k–£5k/month budget · Scales with conversion efficiency
Channel 03 · Partnership
Trade & Industry Partners
The people venue owners already trust - their EHO consultant, food safety trainer, hospitality accountant - are warm referral channels. A trusted advisor recommendation converts faster and sticks longer than any ad.
  • Environmental Health consultant referral programme
  • Hospitality trade bodies (UKHospitality, BII)
  • Food safety training providers (co-marketing)
  • Hospitality-focused accountants and payroll bureaus
  • EPOS and booking system integrations
Investment: Partnership manager time · Revenue share model · Very low CAC
Channel 04 · Product-Led
Free Trial & Referral
The product itself is the best sales tool. A 14-day trial with full feature access removes risk and lets the daily habit form before the ask. Referrals spread inside industry networks - hospitality is a tight-knit community.
  • 14-day free trial - no card required
  • In-app onboarding: live compliance check in 10 minutes
  • Owner referral: one month free per referred venue
  • Group pricing incentive for multi-site operators
  • App Store and Google Play organic discovery
Investment: Product UX investment · Lowest CAC of all channels
Channel mix target (Year 1): 40% organic/content · 30% paid acquisition · 20% partnerships · 10% referral. As referral and partnership momentum builds, paid share reduces.

What drives trial-to-paid conversion
The "aha" moment
Completing a first temperature log with a pass/fail alert, or publishing a rota and seeing staff get notified instantly. Usually happens within 48 hours of signup.
Guided onboarding
In-app checklist walks new users through their first compliance check, rota, and timesheet export. Completion rate predicts conversion within 7 days.
Staff adoption
When team members download the app and start clocking in, the owner sees value from day one - without entering any data themselves.
Price anchoring
£25/month lands well against a £45+ competitor subscription or the cost of one hour of admin time. The comparison is the conversion argument.
Retention strategy
Once a team's compliance records, rota history, and staff files are in Pelikn, the operational habit is embedded into every shift. Churn is low when the product is used daily.

Geographic focus
Northern Ireland first, then mainland UK.
Northern Ireland is our launch market - tighter community, faster word-of-mouth, and the same FSA food hygiene framework as the rest of the UK. A proven product here is the foundation for expansion into Belfast's city venues, then mainland UK cities in Phase 02.
Vertical focus
Restaurants first, then pubs and cafés.
Full-service restaurants have the highest compliance burden and the highest willingness to pay. Pubs and cafés follow in Phase 02 with features tuned to their specific workflows.
