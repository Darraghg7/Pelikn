# Pelikn — Design Context

Hospitality compliance + team-management app (formerly SafeServ). React + Vite + Tailwind, Supabase, Capacitor (iOS/Android). Mobile-first.

## Users
Independent UK hospitality venues & small food groups (cafés, restaurants, bakeries), increasingly any small business. Primary user is a busy owner/manager on a phone; secondary is floor staff (PIN login). They want compliance + team ops in one place — not paperwork.

## Brand personality
Confident, warm, trustworthy. Tagline: "Run your venue. Not paperwork." Calm and credible (it's compliance software) without being cold or corporate.

## Aesthetic direction
- **Type**: Geist (sans) + Geist Mono. Mono is used for labels, numbers, and eyebrows.
- **Color** (tokens in `tailwind.config.js`): brand deep green `#13362a`, coral accent `#c94f2a`, surface `#f3f3ef`, paper `#ffffff`, ink scale `#0d1a14`→`#b3b9b5`, plus good/warn/bad status. Use tokens, never hardcoded hex.
- **Feel**: flat, clean, generous spacing; soft single-elevation shadows; rounded cards; a deliberate mono-uppercase micro-label system.
- Light + dark mode (class-based).

## Design principles
1. Use design tokens, never hardcoded hex.
2. Mobile-first — verify at 375px; it's a Capacitor app.
3. Legible by default, but respect the intentional micro-label aesthetic (see type floor).
4. One elevation scale, one off-white, one brand green.

## Type-size floor standard (calibrated)
- Styled mono-uppercase micro-labels: **11px floor**.
- Body / content text: **12px floor** (`text-xs`).
- Nudge contrast on faint small labels (e.g. `text-white/40` → `/55` on dark cards).
- **Exception — space-constrained grids** (dashboard 3-col stat tiles, rota week grid): do NOT force the floor; keep labels compact enough to avoid wrapping. The rota week grid is an accepted, intentional dense view — keep it close to current; don't redesign or force the floor.
